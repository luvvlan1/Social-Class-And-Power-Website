The OuterBanks

What is the outerbanks?

"Outer Banks" is a teen drama and adventure series on Netflix created by Josh Pate, Jonas Pate, and Shannon Burke. The show premiered on April 15, 2020, and quickly gained a huge fanbase for its thrilling plot, scenic setting, and engaging characters.

It portrays the divide between two main groups:

  • The Pogues: Working-class teenagers who live on the "wrong side of the tracks" and spend their days fishing, working odd jobs, and seeking adventure.

  • The Kooks: Wealthy, privileged residents who live lavish lifestyles and look down on the Pogues.

    • This class conflict is central to the story, creating tension between the two groups.

    What does the OBX show consist of?

    The series follows a group of Pogues led by John B Routledge (played by Chase Stokes). When John B’s father disappears mysteriously, he and his friends set out to uncover the truth, leading them on a treasure hunt tied to a legendary fortune and dark secrets.



    The show combines elements of action, romance, and mystery as the Pogues face dangerous adversaries, uncover betrayals, and navigate their relationships—all while racing against the Kooks and other enemies in search of treasure.
